Final Project Proposal!

Project Concept/Motivation

I want to build a motion-activated LED strip that lights up when I walk down the stairs at night. The LEDs will follow step by step as I move up or down. If this approach doesn’t work as intended, I will have the LED strip be activated by another method and slowly fade out. Additionally, I will integrate an LCD screen to display messages. My motivation for this project comes from my own experience—whenever I go downstairs at night, I always have to turn the lights on and off manually. With this system, the stairway lighting will be more efficient, automated, and convenient.

Anticipated Bill of Materials

  • LED strip
  • Motion Sensor (ultrasonic sonar sensor HC-SR04)
  • Arduino
  • Battery pack (for wireless use)
  • MOSFET
  • Soldering wires to the LED strip
  • Wires & Resistors

Timeline and Contingency Plans

Week 1: I will test individual components to ensure they function correctly. This includes:

  • Testing the LED strips (checking brightness and functionality), adjusting them to my preferences.
  • Testing the motion sensor (detection range and response time).
  • Testing the LCD screen (ensuring it works and displays messages correctly).
  • Working on the code to ensure the LEDs turn on when motion is detected, and replacing any components that don’t work.

Week 2: I will create something to hide the breadboard and wires while still allowing the motion sensor to detect movement. I will also:

  • Ensure that the wires and battery are correctly placed and functioning.
  • Improve the LED transition effects and debug any coding issues.

If something goes wrong with this plan, such as the LED strips not working, I will check the wiring for issues or replace the LED strip if necessary. My biggest concern is the motion sensor, as I have not worked with it before. If the sensor doesn’t work as expected, I will check its placement and adjust its sensitivity. If issues continue, I might try using a different type of sensor.
